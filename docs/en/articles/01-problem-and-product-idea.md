# Problem and Product Idea

## Starting problem

Emby libraries often grow over years. They contain movies, shows, favorites, partially watched seasons and content that matters to different users. The real problem is not lack of media, but the decision at the moment of watching.

Typical situations:

- Users do not want to search for a long time.
- Favorite lists exist, but do not feel like a running channel.
- Shows are started and then disappear back into the catalog.
- Live TV is convenient, but not personal.
- Manual VirtualTV channels are tedious to maintain.

## Product idea

Emby UserTV Stream turns personal signals into a personal live channel. Ideally, the user opens Emby, sees their own channel and starts watching.

The channel is not a classic broadcaster. It is automatically maintained VirtualTV programming generated from local Emby media.

## Guiding principles

1. **User-specific instead of global:** every user gets different content.
2. **Reuse existing signals:** favorites and playback history are reused.
3. **Lean-back instead of searching:** the channel should be easy to start.
4. **Do not destroy metadata:** media, tags and favorites are not rewritten.
5. **Controlled automation:** only clearly managed playlists and channels are changed.

## Boundary

This project is a private experiment. It is not released as an official product, official Emby plugin or commercial solution. The documentation explains what was built and why, not what is guaranteed to work.
